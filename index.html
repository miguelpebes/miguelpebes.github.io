Curriculum Vitae


I am a Research Fellow in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science in the School of Materials Science and Engineering at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. I received my Ph.D. in Statistical Science in 2023 from Indiana University Bloomington (IUB), where I was co-advised by Daniel Manrique-Vallier and Andrew J. Womack. At IUB, I also Ph.D.-minored in Complex Networks and Systems, and served as a Visting Assistant Professor at the Department of Statistics from 2022 to 2023. I received my M.S. in Statistics and B.S. in Computer Science from the Pontifical Catholic University of Peru.

My research integrates statistical modeling and computational inference methods for understanding and controllability of real-world systems. I have worked in various domains including telecommunications, fraud detection, pattern-change detection, mobility analysis, risk management, hidden hierarchy and network reconstruction, and currently remote sensing and precision agriculture. My technical core includes Bayesian modeling, Bayesian Nonparametrics, Markov Chain Monte Carlo algorithms, and diachronic applications of these methods resembling Reinforcement Learning.
